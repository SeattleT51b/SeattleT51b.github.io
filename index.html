Parts & Shopping List

  • Fallout 76 Collector's Edition helmet, outfitted with Henry's Helmet Fans and powered by cheap batteries
  • Many rolls of EVA Foam 10mm (at least 19.5sqft) and 5mm (at least 112sqft)
  • DAP Contact Cement Weldwood (a lot) (srsly, so much)
  • Moldable Foam Clay, for smoothing gaps
  • Cheap black tactical/motorcycle gloves
  • Suspenders, to hold the upper leg armor in place
  • VELCRO® Brand, 2" strips, sew-on white and black, several feet, to hold many other parts in place
  • "Armor" look achieved with Plasti Dip GunMetal Gray 11oz spray cans, a full case, silver automotive spray paint, various other spray paints
  • Dr. Martens boots
  • Power Armor Assault Rifle 3D printed in Canada by Monkey Mila. The rifle is 1.672m, or in Freedom Units, 5'6"ft tall.
  • Fallout-Inspired Fusion Core Container 3D printed from Etsy (does not actually produce electricity)
  • Fallout Power Armor Fusion Core Wheel 3D print
  • T-51b power armor EVA Foam templates by Amazing3DPapercraft on Etsy, tho, see above as this was a very challenging start that's hard to recommend.
  • Heat gun for sealing/texturing the foam (but not too much!), random hardware store hoses, lots of razor blades, scissors big and small, a Dremel rotary tool, and a lot of patience.
